Guinevere Beck

How Guinevere and Joe Met

Joe meets Beck while working at a bookstore in New York. Their interaction seems casual—a fleeting exchange over a book—but to Joe, it is electric. He is immediately captivated by her intelligence, charm, vulnerability, and ambition as a writer. Beck represents an idealized puzzle to him: someone he believes can be understood, shaped, and ultimately “protected.” Almost instantly, his curiosity turns into obsession. He begins meticulously researching her online, following her routines, tracking her social media, and learning about her friends and romantic connections. To Joe, every small detail becomes a piece of the image he is constructing of the perfect relationship.

Even at this early stage, Joe’s thoughts reveal the duality of his fascination. While outwardly he appears charming and helpful, internally he is already planning how to insert himself into Beck’s life. The line between affection and control is blurred in his mind; his actions, though seemingly thoughtful, are motivated by a desire to dominate her world before she even knows him fully. Beck, oblivious to the depth of his obsession, perceives Joe as kind, intelligent, and attentive—a potential confidant or lover, someone whose interest in her feels flattering and sincere.

The Obsession

Joe’s fascination quickly escalates into full-blown obsession. He orchestrates “chance” encounters with Beck, deliberately engineering opportunities to be near her, to appear in her life as a helpful and caring presence. He rationalizes his invasive behavior as acts of love, seeing himself as the only one capable of keeping her safe from harm. People in Beck’s life—her boyfriend, Benji, and certain friends—become targets of his scrutiny. Joe convinces himself that removing these influences is a protective gesture, though in reality it is an assertion of control.

The Relationship

When Joe and Beck officially begin dating, the cracks in the fantasy start to emerge. Beck is a real, flawed person: insecure, independent, and occasionally dishonest. Instead of embracing these traits, Joe grows increasingly frustrated, viewing them as obstacles to the idealized relationship he has imagined. What once began as admiration gradually transforms into a desire to control and mold her into the perfect partner.

Joe’s love becomes conditional. He manipulates situations to limit Beck’s autonomy and isolates her from people he perceives as threats to their connection. Small disagreements and inconsistencies in her behavior trigger disproportionate jealousy, reinforcing his belief that he must manage every aspect of her life. While Beck experiences genuine affection for Joe, she also begins sensing that something is not right, though she cannot fully comprehend the depths of his surveillance and control.

The Collapse

The relationship collapses when Beck discovers evidence of Joe’s crimes and the hidden glass cage where he has imprisoned previous victims. Her attempts to manipulate Joe emotionally to survive briefly delay the inevitable, but Joe’s obsessive need to maintain his narrative of love and control overrides any hesitation. In a tragic culmination of obsession, fear, and possessiveness, he strangles Beck, believing he is preserving their bond even as he destroys it.
